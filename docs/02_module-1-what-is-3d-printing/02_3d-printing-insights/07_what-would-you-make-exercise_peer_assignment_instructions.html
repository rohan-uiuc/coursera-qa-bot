This first exercise is designed to get you to think about making. By doing this, I am hoping to stimulate your imagination. Being able to imagine yourself as a maker is the most important aspect of the entire 3D printing process because in order to make something, you first need to imagine it.

This assignment will require you to use a video camera. You can just use a web camera on your computer, but other methods are also acceptable.

  1. Access your video camera (on your computer, laptop, or phone) and record a video of yourself.

  2. Tell us your name and where you are from.

  3. Tell us what you would like to make using 3D printing technology.

  4. Tell us why you want to make this.

  5. Record and post your video by following the instructions below, then post the URL of your video on the My submission tab.

You may save a draft of your work as you go, and you can come back later to continue working on your draft. When you are finished working, click the Preview button, verify your identity, and then click Submit for review to submit the assignment.

Review criteria

You are required to evaluate the submissions of at least THREE of your peers based on the instructions and rubric provided. You may begin giving feedback to other students as soon as you submit your assignment; click the Review classmates tab to begin. Feel free to provide additional reviews beyond the three required!

Every submission will need to include the following:

  1. Your name

  2. What you would make

  3. Why you want to make this

Each of the three parts of the submission (see above) will be graded on the following scale:

  • 0 points: Does not provide the required information

  • 1 point: Provides the required information

Note: To pass this assignment, you need a total score of 3 points .

How to Record and Post Your Video

The steps below describe how to record, upload, and submit your video for this exercise.

If you would like to keep your video private and visible only to other learners in this course, pay special attention to steps 7 and 8. If not, we plan to showcase some Learner stories on our social media platforms and on our website .

  1. Record your short video introduction using a computer camera, cell phone camera, or any other recording device that will allow you to save a digital video to your computer. If you are using a cell phone, record the video holding the longer side of the phone parallel to the ground(landscape mode).

  2. Save your video to your computer.

  3. Go to http://youtube.com and create an account, if you don't already have one. If you do not wish to be identified, create a new account with a pseudonym. (Note: for those users who cannot access YouTube, there are a number of other video sites that will work for this assignment, such as ToDou or Youku in China. However, the rest of these instructions are specific to YouTube.)

  4. Sign in, and then click on the Upload button at the top of the page.

  5. Click on the "Select files to upload" in the middle of the screen and then locate and click on your video to upload it.

  6. Go to your "Video Manager" page.

  7. Select the video you want to make “unlisted”. Click the "Edit" button to access the video's settings.

  8. Go to the Privacy section of the page. There you'll see the option to mark your video as "unlisted", "public", or "private." Select "unlisted". By selecting "unlisted," your video will be hosted by YouTube but cannot be found by searching on YouTube; however, it will be accessible on Coursera once you embed a link. Be sure not to selecting "private", as that will prevent your classmates from seeing and reviewing your video. If you have problems with setting your video as "unlisted,”, see YouTube's help page.

  9. Click the "Save Changes" button. Once you've done this, your video will be an "unlisted" video. This is available only to people in this class. Please respect the privacy of others; do not share other people's URLs unless you have their written permission!

  10. Copy the URL from your video and paste it into the URL field of the My submission tab.

  11. (optional) If you would like to share your story with other learners on social media, use the URL and visit any of our social media websites and share.

  12. Give your project a title and click on the Save or Preview button at the bottom of the page.

If you have trouble with platforms other than YouTube, please look at the support pages for the individual platform or post a message in the discussion thread setup for video troubleshooting.

Please remember, the assignment should be in English to make it easier for your peer reviewer (who might be from any country in the world) to grade.

Have fun!
